# High-Fidelity Fuel Targeting and Deterministic Combustion

**Adapting EUV Lithography Tin Droplet Architectures to Advanced Marine and Motorsport Propulsion**

[![License: MIT](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-MIT-yellow.svg)](LICENSE)

---

## Abstract

This engineering feasibility report investigates the potential of transposing the precision fluid dynamics and optical targeting mechanisms employed in Extreme Ultraviolet (EUV) lithography — specifically the ASML Laser-Produced Plasma (LPP) source — to the domain of internal combustion engines (ICE).

The core inquiry assesses whether the engineering principles used to align and vaporize molten tin droplets with nanosecond precision can be adapted to achieve "exact entry" of fuel into the combustion chamber, theoretically maximising combustion efficiency, extending the lean-burn limit, and eliminating particulate emissions through deterministic rather than stochastic fuel delivery.

The analysis synthesises data from semiconductor manufacturing, fluid dynamics, quantum chemistry, and advanced propulsion engineering. It delineates a critical distinction between molecular alignment (controlling the quantum axis of individual molecules) and droplet alignment (controlling the spatial trajectory of fuel mass). While the thermodynamic chaos of an ICE renders quantum molecular alignment unfeasible for hydrocarbons, the application of Rayleigh–Plateau deterministic droplet generation presents a revolutionary pathway for combustion technology.

---

## Key Propositions

- **Digital Combustion** — replacing stochastic high-pressure fuel sprays with piezo-modulated, laser-targeted monodisperse droplet streams
- **F1 Application** — a digital pre-chamber using MEMS-scale Rayleigh–Plateau nozzles and fibre-coupled laser ignition, extending the lean-burn limit of sustainable fuels within the 100 kg/hr mass flow constraint
- **Marine Application** — laser-assisted secondary atomisation of Heavy Fuel Oil (HFO), shattering large droplets in-cylinder to reduce soot and enable future fuels (ammonia, methanol)
- **LIBS Diagnostics** — real-time Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy for in-cylinder fuel quality sensing and catalytic fine detection
